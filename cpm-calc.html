Understanding Digital Advertising Metrics

Digital marketing runs on data. Whether you are running Facebook Ads, buying banner space, or analyzing your AdSense revenue, three acronyms rule them all: CPM, CPC, and CTR. Understanding the relationship between these numbers is the key to lowering your ad costs and increasing profit.

1. What is CPM? (Cost Per Mille)

"Mille" is Latin for thousand. CPM measures how much it costs to show your ad 1,000 times.

CPM = (Total Cost / Impressions) Ã— 1,000

Why it matters: CPM is the industry standard for brand awareness campaigns. A lower CPM means you are reaching more people for less money.

2. What is CPC? (Cost Per Click)

CPC tells you exactly how much you pay every time a user clicks your ad. This is the most critical metric for performance marketers driving traffic to a landing page.

CPC = Total Cost / Total Clicks

3. What is CTR? (Click Through Rate)

CTR is the percentage of people who see your ad and decide to click on it. It measures the "quality" or "relevance" of your ad creative.
