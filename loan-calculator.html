How Loan Amortization Works

Amortization is the mathematical process of spreading out a loan into a series of fixed payments. While your total monthly payment remains the same, the components change over time:

1. The Interest Heavy Phase: In the beginning, the majority of your payment goes toward paying off interest. This is how banks make their money upfront.
2. The Tipping Point: About halfway through the loan term, the balance shifts.
3. The Principal Phase: In the final years, almost 100% of your payment goes toward reducing the actual debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does paying extra really help?

Yes! Because interest is calculated on your *remaining balance*, every extra dollar you pay reduces the principal immediately. This means interest has less balance to grow on next month. It creates a snowball effect that can save you thousands.

Q: Can I use this for Auto Loans?

Absolutely. Select a shorter term (e.g., 5 or 7 years) to calculate car payments. The math remains the same for any fixed-rate loan.

Q: What is a good interest rate?

Interest rates fluctuate based on the economy and your credit score. As of 2024/2025, a typical mortgage rate is between 6% and 8%, while personal loans can range from 8% to 15%.
