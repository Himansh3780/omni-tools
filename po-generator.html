Purchase Order vs. Invoice: What is the difference?

This is the most common question in business accounting. Here is the simple breakdown:

  • Purchase Order (PO): Sent by the Buyer to the Seller at the start. It lists what you want to buy and the price you agree to pay. It is a "Request to Buy."
  • Invoice: Sent by the Seller to the Buyer at the end. It requests payment for the goods that were delivered. It is a "Request for Payment."

Why do you need a PO?

1. Legal Protection: Once a vendor accepts your PO, it becomes a legally binding contract. If they deliver the wrong items or change the price later, you have the PO as proof of the original agreement.
2. Budget Control: POs help businesses track upcoming expenses before the invoice even arrives.
3. Professionalism: Suppliers prioritize orders that come with an official PO number because it guarantees they will get paid.
