What are Meta Tags and Why Do They Matter?

Meta tags are snippets of text that describe a page's content. They don't appear on the page itself, but only in the page's source code. They are essentially little content descriptors that help tell search engines what a web page is about.

Key Meta Tags

  • Title Tag: The most important tag for SEO. It appears as the clickable headline in search results. Optimal Length: 50-60 characters.
  • Meta Description: A brief summary of the page. Google uses this for the snippet displayed below the title. Optimal Length: 150-160 characters.
  • Viewport: Critical for mobile responsiveness. Without this, phones will render your site like a desktop page (tiny text).
  • Robots: Tells Google ("crawlers") whether they are allowed to index your page or follow links on it.
