What is RoAS? (Return on Ad Spend)

RoAS stands for Return on Ad Spend. It is the most critical metric for online marketers, dropshippers, and e-commerce store owners. It tells you exactly how much money you make for every dollar you spend on advertising.

The RoAS Formula

Calculating RoAS is simple division:

RoAS = Total Conversion Revenue / Total Ad Spend

Example: If you spend $1,000 on Facebook Ads and generate $4,000 in sales:
$4,000 / $1,000 = 4.0 RoAS (or "4X").

What is a "Good" RoAS?

  • Under 2.0x: Generally considered poor or break-even, depending on your profit margins.
  • 3.0x - 4.0x: Healthy profitability. This is the goal for most e-commerce brands.
  • Over 5.0x: Exceptional performance ("Unicorn" status). Scale your budget immediately!

RoAS vs. ROI: What's the difference?

RoAS only looks at the efficiency of your Ad Spend. ROI (Return on Investment) accounts for all expenses (product cost, shipping, employees, etc.). You can have a positive RoAS but a negative ROI if your product costs are too high.
