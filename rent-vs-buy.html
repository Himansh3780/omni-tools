Should You Rent or Buy in 2026?

The decision to rent an apartment or buy a home is one of the biggest financial choices you will make. While buying builds equity, it comes with hidden monthly costs like Property Tax, HOA fees, and Maintenance. Renting offers flexibility but no long-term asset value.

Our Rent vs. Buy Calculator compares the immediate monthly cash flow difference. It calculates your estimated Mortgage Payment (Principal + Interest) and adds estimated taxes to give you a fair "Apples to Apples" comparison against your monthly rent.

Key Factors to Consider

  • Upfront Costs: Buying requires a Down Payment (usually 3.5% to 20%) and Closing Costs. Renting usually requires just a Security Deposit.
  • Maintenance: When you rent, the landlord fixes the leaking roof. When you own, that $5,000 repair bill is yours.
  • Equity: Every mortgage payment pays down your debt and builds ownership. Rent payments are gone forever.

How is the Monthly Buying Cost Calculated?

We use the standard mortgage formula:

Monthly Cost = (Principal & Interest) + (Yearly Tax / 12) + (Home Insurance / 12)

This provides a realistic view of what actually leaves your bank account every month, not just the loan payment.
