US Dollar Inflation Calculator (CPI Data)

"In my day, a movie ticket cost a nickel!" We have all heard it. But exactly how much has the value of money changed? Our Inflation Calculator uses historical Consumer Price Index (CPI) data to measure the purchasing power of the dollar over time.

How does this work?

We use the official Consumer Price Index (CPI) published by the US Bureau of Labor Statistics. The CPI tracks the price of a "basket of goods" (food, housing, transport) across different years.

The Formula:
Value in Future Dollars = Value in Past Dollars Ã— (CPI Future / CPI Past)

Why does Inflation happen?

  • Demand-Pull: When demand for goods exceeds supply, prices rise.
  • Cost-Push: When the cost of production (wages, materials) increases, companies raise prices.
  • Monetary Expansion: When the government prints more money, the value of each existing dollar decreases.
