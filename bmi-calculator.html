Verified Medical Information

The Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening tool used by the World Health Organization (WHO) and medical professionals globally to estimate the amount of body fat. While not a direct measure of body fat, it correlates with direct measures and is the standard for health risk assessment.

BMI Categories Chart

Classification BMI Range (kg/m²) Health Risk
Underweight Below 18.5 Malnutrition Risk
Healthy Weight 18.5 - 24.9 Low Risk
Overweight 25.0 - 29.9 Increased Risk
Obesity (Class I) 30.0 - 34.9 High Risk

How is Ideal Weight Calculated?

Our tool uses the Reverse BMI Method. We calculate the weight range that would place you exactly between a BMI of 18.5 and 24.9 for your specific height. This is considered the "Healthy Zone" for most adults.

⚠️ Disclaimer: BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes or bodybuilders may classify as "Overweight" due to muscle mass. Always consult a doctor for a full health assessment.
