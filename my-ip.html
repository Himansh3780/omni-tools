Understanding IP Addresses and Privacy

An IP (Internet Protocol) Address is a unique string of numbers separated by periods that identifies each computer using the Internet Protocol to communicate over a network. Think of it as your computer's "home address" on the web.

Public vs. Private IP

Most devices have two IP addresses:

  • Private IP: Used inside your home network (e.g., 192.168.1.5). This is how your phone talks to your printer. It is not visible to the outside world.
  • Public IP: Assigned by your ISP (Internet Service Provider). This is the address websites see when you visit them. This tool shows your Public IP.

IPv4 vs. IPv6

We are running out of IP addresses! The old standard, IPv4 (e.g., 192.0.2.1), allows for 4.3 billion unique addresses. The new standard, IPv6 (e.g., 2001:0db8:85a3...), allows for 340 undecillion addresses, solving the shortage.

Why hide your IP?

Your IP address reveals your approximate physical location (City/State) and your ISP. Hackers or advertisers can use this data to track your online activity. Using a VPN (Virtual Private Network) masks your real IP, replacing it with one from a secure server.
